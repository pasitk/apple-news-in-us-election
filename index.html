Duke St. Arcade: Place with highest risk of bike theft in Cardiff city centre

Pasit Kongkunakornkul, Cardiff University

8 January 2020

The data from UK Police reveals that there were an average 41 bikes were stolen per month around Cardiff city centre and some nearby areas during January to November 2019. It demonstrates that the total number of bike theft is 452 bikes, and the place with highest risk is ‘On or Near’ Duke Street Arcade as shown in the map below.

This information was gathered from the UK police website data.police.uk, and mainly focused on the area of Cardiff city centre and nearby areas like Bute Park, Cardiff City Hall, The Welsh Government Offices, and Cardiff University Main Hall.

In the area of Duke Street Arcade, there were 63 bikes stolen in 11 months; about 6 bikes per month. The area outside a nightclub on Wharton street had 41 bikes stolen from it, and the area on Wood street near the Millennium Stadium had 36 bikes stolen.

The correspondent surveyed bike racks in front of Duke Street Arcade and found that there are some CCTV cameras around the area. However, none of them was turned directly to capture the bike racks. In addition, the bike racks behind the museum of Cardiff near Wharton street has no camera pointed directly to them as well. While there are some cameras pointed directly to bike racks in front of JOMEC and Vue cinema building.

There are also other points in Cardiff city centre where bikes were frequently stolen such as Queen Street, Windsor Lane, or near the nightclub close to the Old Arcade.

The statistics also indicate that the period starting from April saw a dramatic growth in the number of bikes stolen. It reached the highest number in August, when there were 58 bikes stolen around the focused areas mentioned above, before dropping sharply until November.

The area on Wood street, which is ranked third amongst places where most of bicycles were stolen, is also near the school of Journalism, Media, and Culture (JOMEC) of Cardiff University. Linh Vuong, a Vietnamese postgraduate student at JOMEC lost her second-hand bike in this area too. She said that in the day she lost her bike, she parked the bike in front of JOMEC building in the morning and felt confident that she already locked her bike. But when she returned to pick her bike at around 17:00 on the same day, the bike was gone. Nowadays she has decided to walk instead of buying a new bike, but worries that this will limit her ability to travel further away.

“If you go along the street, you’ll still find lots of cases that even though they have the D-Lock (the best type of lock), They still lost their bikes. What the security officer told me is that they need the frame of a bike for reselling”

However, since the police have contacted the building’s security staff and requested for a CCTV footage video, she has not heard anything about the progress of her case. She expects that the police should update the progress of the cases to all victims of bicycle theft including her and raise awareness about bike parking safety.

An old D-Lock which is left with a bicycle rack in Cardiff's Queen Street

While Anna Hammond, Press Officer from Cardiff Bay Police Station, said that there are several factors which increase the risk of bike theft such as leaving bicycles either insecure or secure with a low-quality lock, or bike parking in the area which is not covered by CCTV. Nevertheless, she said that the number of bikes stolen reached a peak in August because it is the month that the highest number of people use a bike. Furthermore, the weather in that month which is quite nice may encourage people to use the bicycle more.

She also explained the police’s measures to prevent bicycle theft: “We patrol the areas where the bicycle racks are and the team who when they do patrol, they will patrol those areas. But a big part of it is getting the messages out there, the crime prevention messages, asking people to properly secure their bikes.”

The South Wales Police also provide some guidance for citizens to avoid their bicycles from being stolen. For example, to lock the bike: Use a Sold Secure bike lock and ensure that the owners lock their bike everywhere they park it, lock the bike at a purpose-built bike rack as it may be removed from other items of street furniture, lock both the frame & wheels to the bike rack, and so on. Bicycle theft stands in the sixth rank of the most frequently happened crime types in the area within 11 months. In addition, there are also other crime types which are in the top rank. Starting from ‘Anti-social behaviour’ which is the most frequently occurred. It covers many kinds of activities such as nuisance, vandalism, graffiti, flyposting, street drinking, etc. There were 1,433 cases of them. The second rank is ‘Violence and Sexual Offences’ with 1,389 cases and the third rank is ‘Shoplifting’ with 1,123 cases within 11 months.
